Dhanteras

Dhanteras marks the beginning of the five days long Diwali celebrations. worship Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth to bless them with prosperity in the coming year. They also worship Kuber the treasurer of wealth. It is said that buying gold jewellery and new utensils on this day helps bring good fortune.

Narak Chaturdashi

The second day is celebrated as Naraka Chaturdashi across Tamil Nadu, Goa and Karnataka. According to mythology, it is the day that Lord Krishna slayed the tyrant demon Narkasura.

Lakshmi Puja

The third day is the main day of the festival, and coincides with the last day of the dark fortnight of the lunar month. Diwali is the day when feasts are cooked, houses are decorated with colourful rangolis at the entrance, cheerful torans on the doorway and the dark night is lit aglow with earthen oil lamps (diyas), truly making it a festival of lights.

Goverdhan Puja

The day after Diwali is celebrated as Goverdhan Puja by some communities of the north, west and central regions, honouring the legend of the Hindu god Krishna who lifted the Goverdhan mountain to save the cowherd and farming communities from incessant rains and floods triggered by Lord Indra's anger.

Bhai Duj

The last or the 5th day of the festivities is marked by celebrating Bhai Duj (brother's day). It is also known as Bhau Beej, Bhai Tilak or Bhai Phonta.It celebrates the sacred bond between a brother and sister.
